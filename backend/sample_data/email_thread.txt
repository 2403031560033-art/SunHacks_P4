Subject: RE: Database Migration Strategy - Final Decision
Date: 2024-02-20

Team,

After extensive evaluation over the past two weeks, we have decided to migrate from MongoDB to PostgreSQL for our primary data store. Here is the complete reasoning behind this decision:

1. Our data has become increasingly relational as the product matured, with complex joins needed across 12+ collections
2. PostgreSQL offers better ACID compliance for our financial transactions - this is critical for SOC2 compliance
3. The JSONB support in PostgreSQL gives us the flexibility we need for semi-structured data without sacrificing query performance
4. Cost savings of approximately 40% on hosting - managed PostgreSQL at $8K/month vs MongoDB Atlas at $13.5K/month

Alternatives considered:
- Staying with MongoDB: Rejected because of increasing complexity in managing cross-collection relationships and rising costs
- Moving to MySQL: Rejected due to inferior JSON handling and fewer advanced features like CTEs and window functions  
- CockroachDB: Interesting option with great horizontal scaling, but team lacks expertise and community support is still growing
- Amazon DynamoDB: Rejected because it would increase vendor lock-in and doesn't support complex queries well

The migration will happen in 3 phases:
Phase 1: Set up PostgreSQL alongside MongoDB with schema design (2 weeks)
Phase 2: Dual-write to both databases with data validation (4 weeks)
Phase 3: Cut over to PostgreSQL and decommission MongoDB (2 weeks)

Key participants in this decision: Jennifer Martinez (Engineering Lead), Tom Wilson (DBA), Rachel Green (Product Manager), and Alex Kumar (Senior Backend Engineer).

The confidence in this decision is high because we ran a 2-week proof of concept that validated all our assumptions about performance and data compatibility. Query performance improved by 3x on average.

Risk mitigation: We will maintain MongoDB backups for 90 days post-migration and have a documented rollback procedure.
