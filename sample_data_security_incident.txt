MEETING TRANSCRIPT - INCIDENT RESPONSE #882
Topic: Unauthorized Database Access Attempt and Authentication Overhaul
Participants: David (CISO), Maria (Lead Security Engineer), James (CTO)

David: Let's get started. Maria, run us through the incident from yesterday night.

Maria: Sure. At roughly 2:00 AM, our SOC flagged multiple anomalous login attempts originating from masked IPs. The attacker was attempting a credential stuffing attack against our custom user-auth microservice. They did not gain access, but it put our CPU load to 98% and almost crashed the auth server.

James: Our custom auth layer is five years old. We've been meaning to replace it. What are our options right now to harden it?

Maria: We could implement rate-limiting via Cloudflare, but the custom logic is still vulnerable to logic bypassing if they find a 0-day. Alternatively, we finally rip out the custom auth and migrate to Auth0 or Okta. 

David: Okta is enterprise-grade but configuring it across our 12 legacy sub-domains will take 3 months of implementation time. We have compliance audits in 4 weeks.

Maria: Auth0 has a drop-in React/Python SDK that we can integrate in about two weeks. It handles JWTs, rate limiting, and brute-force protection out of the box natively. 

James: Okay, given the compliance audit timeline and the recent scare, we cannot rely on our custom code anymore. The decision is to immediately deprecate the internal authentication microservice. We will migrate all user authentication to Auth0 by the end of the sprint. Maria, you are the lead on ensuring SSO compatibility remains intact.

David: Agreed. Closing the incident ticket.
